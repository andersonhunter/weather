FAQ

Q: Where does the app pull its data from?
A: The app pulls historic data from the Open-Meteo weather API, which uses historic weather data from 1940 to the present. This weather data uses ERA5 weather reanalysis implemented by ECMWF. To learn more: Open-Meteo API, Copernicus

Q: What data does the app compute?
A: The app will compute a 5-number summary (Sample minimum, IQR, sample maximum) as well as standard deviation, and average.

Q: How does the app calculate these values?
A: The app uses Python's statistics module. The statistics are accurate to 2 decimal places. In order to circumvent Python's handling of float values, data pulled from the API is rounded to two decimal places, multiplied by 100, and converted to an integer. The app then converts the generated statistics back to float values, preserving the integrity of the data.

Q: What kind of data can the app pull from the API?
A: The app can currently process the maximum, minimum, and average daily temperatures within the user-selected range at the user-selected zip code.

Q: What does the future hold for the application?
A: The app will soon be able to plot the climate data, and handle many different types of weather data. Suggestions are welcome for further features, please submit inquiries below!

Q: Why is a frog behind the FAQ?
A: Thank you for asking! Each of the animals chosen as backgrounds for the pages are currently experiencing population declines due to climate change.
